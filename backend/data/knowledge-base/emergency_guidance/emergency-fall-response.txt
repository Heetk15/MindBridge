# Responding to a Fall

## Immediate Assessment
- Do not immediately move the person.
- Ask if they feel pain, especially in the hips, back, or head.
- Check for bleeding, swelling, or bone deformities.

## When to Call 911
Call for emergency help immediately if:
- They are unconscious or disoriented.
- They cannot move a limb.
- They have severe pain.
- They hit their head.

## Getting Up Safely
If there are no injuries and they feel they can get up:
1. Bring a sturdy chair close to them.
2. Have them roll onto their side, then onto their hands and knees.
3. Instruct them to hold the chair and bring one foot forward to flat on the ground.
4. Assist them gently as they push up to a standing or sitting position.
