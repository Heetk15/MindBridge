# Medical Escalation Procedures

## Knowing Who to Call
Keep a prioritized list of contacts visible:
1. Emergency Services (911)
2. Primary Care Physician
3. Specialist Doctors (e.g., Cardiologist, Neurologist)
4. Nearest Urgent Care Center
5. Designated Family Members

## When to go to the ER vs Urgent Care
- **ER:** Chest pain, difficulty breathing, sudden numbness/weakness, severe bleeding, major head injury.
- **Urgent Care:** Minor cuts needing stitches, minor sprains, fever without other severe symptoms, minor infections.

## Preparing for the Hospital
Keep a "Go Bag" ready containing:
- Current medication list and medical history.
- Insurance cards and ID.
- Advanced directives or DNR orders.
- A change of clothes and basic toiletries.
