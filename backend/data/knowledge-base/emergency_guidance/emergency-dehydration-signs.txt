# Signs of Severe Dehydration

## Why Seniors are at Risk
Seniors often have a reduced sense of thirst and may be on medications (like diuretics) that increase fluid loss.

## Warning Signs
- Extreme thirst (though some may not feel thirsty).
- Very dry mouth and swollen tongue.
- Confusion, dizziness, or lethargy.
- Dark-colored urine or lack of urination for over 8 hours.
- Sunken eyes.

## Action Steps
- Offer clear fluids immediately (water, oral rehydration solutions).
- Do not give sugary or caffeinated drinks.
- If the person is confused, unable to keep fluids down, or extremely weak, seek immediate medical attention.
