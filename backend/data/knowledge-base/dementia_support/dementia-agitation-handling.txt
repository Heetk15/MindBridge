# Managing Agitation in Dementia

## Recognizing the Signs
Agitation often presents as pacing, sleeplessness, or sudden outbursts of anger. It is usually a sign of unmet needs, such as pain, hunger, or needing to use the restroom.

## Immediate Response
- Stay calm and speak in a soft, reassuring tone.
- Give the person space if they are physically agitated, but do not leave them entirely alone.
- Check for physical discomfort (e.g., soiled clothing, fever, or pain).

## Redirection
- Offer a favorite snack or drink.
- Play familiar, soothing music.
- Ask them to help with a simple, repetitive task like folding towels.
