# Handling Medication Refusal

## Identify the Reason
Determine why they are refusing. Is the pill too large to swallow? Does it taste bad? Are they experiencing side effects? Or are they simply confused?

## Solutions
- If swallowing is difficult, ask the pharmacist if the pill can be crushed or if a liquid form is available.
- If they are suspicious, try explaining clearly what the medication is for, or have the doctor explain it during a visit.
- If agitation is the cause, take a break and try again in 15-30 minutes.

## Never Force
Never physically force someone to take medication. If refusal continues and the medication is critical, contact a healthcare professional.
